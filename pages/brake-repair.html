Why Braking System Preventative Maintenance Is Important

When you take care of your vehicle’s braking system it takes care of you. You’ll find that your vehicle stops without hassle every time you depress the brake pedal, which keeps you and your passengers safe while you’re on the road. Regular brake inspections – usually recommended every 20,000 miles – let us take a look at the following crucial brake system parts to ensure optimal vehicle safety:

  • Brake pad depth
  • Brake fluid level
  • Brake fluid purity
  • Brake fluid leaks
  • Brake drum wear
  • Brake rotor wear
  • Brake caliper wear
  • Wheel cylinder wear
  • Master cylinder leaks
  • Brake hose leaks

The sooner we catch a problem with any of these parts the lesser the damage. For example, if your brake pad depth is low, we will replace the pads to prevent them from wearing down completely and grinding against your rotors. If they do begin to grind against your rotors, they will damage them and both the pads and rotors will need to be replaced instead of just the pads.
